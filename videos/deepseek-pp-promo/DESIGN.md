## Style Prompt

DeepSeek++ should feel like a polished Chinese browser workspace rather than a generic AI launch reel: light, crisp, local-first, with precise interface screenshots and a calm product-demo cadence. The whale logo and existing extension screenshots define the visual identity, so the video uses a bright porcelain canvas, DeepSeek blue as the main action color, restrained green/orange status accents, and dark ink typography. Motion should feel like panels assembling into a usable workspace: push transitions, focus pulls, small data ticks, and screen cards sliding into place.

## Colors

- `#f7f9ff` canvas background
- `#101827` primary ink text
- `#4f6cff` DeepSeek++ blue accent
- `#dfe7ff` soft blue surface
- `#16a34a` ready/success accent
- `#f59e0b` highlight accent

## Typography

- Chinese display and UI/body: `PingFang SC`, used for Chinese product claims, descriptions, labels, and captions.
- Latin lockup: `Oswald`, used only for the final DeepSeek++ product name.
- Data labels: `JetBrains Mono`, used for MCP/tool/status readouts.

## What NOT to Do

- Do not use purple-blue gradient hero sections or generic neon AI styling.
- Do not hide the product behind abstract stock-like visuals; screenshots must be visible.
- Do not expose internal API paths, protocols, or implementation details.
- Do not turn the video into a flat feature list; keep the main story as a browser-based AI agent workspace.
